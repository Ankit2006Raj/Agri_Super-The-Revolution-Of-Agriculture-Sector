Soil Knowledge Center

Understanding your soil is the foundation of successful farming. Learn about soil types, testing, and improvement techniques.

Soil Types and Characteristics

Soil Type Characteristics Best Crops Management Tips
Sandy Soil Light, warm, dry, tends to be acidic and low in nutrients Carrots, Potatoes, Radishes Add organic matter, mulch to retain moisture
Clay Soil Heavy, cold, nutrient-rich, drains slowly Late season crops, Wheat, Corn Add organic matter, avoid working when wet
Silt Soil Fertile, light but moisture-retentive, easily compacted Most vegetables, Fruit trees Add organic matter, avoid compaction
Loam Soil Perfect balance of sand, silt and clay Most crops Maintain with regular organic matter

Soil Testing

Regular soil testing helps you understand your soil's needs and avoid over-application of fertilizers.

What We Test For:
  • pH Level (Acidity/Alkalinity)
  • Nutrient Levels (N-P-K)
  • Organic Matter Content
  • Micronutrient Levels
  • Soil Structure

Soil Health Improvement Techniques

Cover Cropping

Plant cover crops during off-seasons to prevent erosion, add organic matter, and improve soil structure.

Recommended Cover Crops: Clover, Rye, Buckwheat

Composting

Add compost to improve soil structure, fertility, and microbial activity.

Benefits: Adds nutrients, improves water retention, enhances soil life

Crop Rotation

Rotate crops to prevent nutrient depletion and break pest/disease cycles.

Rotation Example: Legumes → Leafy Greens → Root Crops → Fruiting Crops

Soil pH Management

Soil pH affects nutrient availability to plants. Most crops prefer a slightly acidic to neutral pH (6.0-7.0).

To Raise pH:
  • Add agricultural lime
  • Add wood ash (carefully)
  • Use dolomite lime for Mg deficiency
To Lower pH:
  • Add elemental sulfur
  • Use acidic organic matter
  • Apply aluminum sulfate
